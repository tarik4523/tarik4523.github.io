Tarikul Islam

I am a Graduate Research and Teaching Assistant in the Sonny Astani Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Southern California, working under Dr. Thomas Petersen. I also hold a position as Assistant Professor (on leave) in the Department of Civil Engineering at Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology (AUST), Dhaka, Bangladesh.

My current research focuses on the influence of electrochemical forces on the mobilization and transport of clay-stabilized Pickering emulsions (PEs) in microfluidic porous media. This interdisciplinary study bridges chemo-poromechanics, fluid mechanics, transport phenomena, material science, and civil engineering — with the aim of optimizing waterflooding strategies for enhanced oil recovery by leveraging electrochemical interactions.

I earned my B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from AUST (2017) and M.Sc. in Civil Engineering (Structural) from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) (2022), where my research involved the experimental investigation of boiler blast loads on building structures. I have over 7 years of teaching experience and have authored 10+ peer-reviewed journal articles on topics including fiber-reinforced concrete, structural retrofitting, and numerical modeling of RC structures.

In my leisure time, I enjoy music, films, soccer, and cricket.

research
Current · USC
Electrochemical Transport in Porous Media
Mobilization and transport of clay-stabilized Pickering emulsions in microfluidic porous media, optimizing waterflooding for enhanced oil recovery via electrochemical interactions.
Structural
Blast Load Effects on Structures
Experimental investigation of boiler blast loads on building structures — large- and small-scale testing, data acquisition, structural resilience and safety analysis.
Materials
Fiber-Reinforced & Sustainable Concrete
Mechanical behavior of concrete with natural/synthetic fibers (jute, PVC, banana, coconut) and supplementary cementitious materials (fly ash, rice husk, SCBA, wood powder).
Computational
Numerical Modeling of RC Structures
Modeling and validation of lateral behavior of masonry-infilled RC frames, blast-induced column response (Abaqus), and retrofitting of RC columns under lateral loads.
