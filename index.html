Research

Current · USC
Electrochemical Transport in Porous Media
My current research focuses on the influence of electrochemical forces on the mobilization and transport of clay-stabilized Pickering emulsions (PEs) in microfluidic porous media. This interdisciplinary study bridges chemo-poromechanics, fluid mechanics, transport phenomena, material science, and civil engineering — with the aim of optimizing waterflooding strategies for enhanced oil recovery by leveraging electrochemical interactions.
M.Sc. Research · BUET
Blast Load Effects on Building Structures
My master's research involved the experimental investigation of boiler blast loads on structures, addressing critical concerns related to the resilience and safety of buildings under blast conditions. This work provided valuable experience in solving real-world engineering challenges, conducting both large- and small-scale experimental tests, and performing comprehensive data acquisition and analysis.
Materials Research
Fiber-Reinforced & Sustainable Concrete
Extensive experimental research on the mechanical behavior of concrete incorporating natural and synthetic fibers (jute, PVC, banana, coconut), supplementary cementitious materials (fly ash, rice husk, sugarcane bagasse ash, wood powder), and admixtures. Studies span compressive strength, split tensile behavior, flexural performance, and fresh properties of self-compacting concrete.
Structural Engineering
RC Frame & Column Behavior
Numerical modeling and validation of lateral behavior of masonry-infilled RC frames with focus on sliding failure mechanisms. Parametric analysis of blast-induced column response using Abaqus. Research on retrofitting of RC columns under lateral loads, with experimental and computational components to develop effective rehabilitation strategies for existing structures.
