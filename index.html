Tarikul Islam
Ph.D. Candidate · Civil & Environmental Engineering · University of Southern California

I am a Graduate Research and Teaching Assistant in the Sonny Astani Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Southern California, working under Dr. Thomas Petersen. I also hold a position as Assistant Professor (on leave) in the Department of Civil Engineering at Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology (AUST), Dhaka, Bangladesh.

My current research focuses on the fabrication of bicontinuous cement composites with multifunctional structural and electrical properties — an interdisciplinary study that bridges materials science, electrochemistry, and civil engineering.

I earned my B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from AUST (2017) and M.Sc. in Civil Engineering (Structural) from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) (2022), where my research involved the experimental investigation of boiler blast loads on building structures. I have over 7 years of teaching experience and have authored 10+ peer-reviewed journal articles on fiber-reinforced concrete, structural retrofitting, and numerical modeling.

In my leisure time, I enjoy music, films, soccer, and cricket.

Current Research — USC
Current · Petersen Lab · USC
Fabrication of Bicontinuous Cement Composites with Multifunctional Structural and Electrical Properties
This research focuses on engineering bicontinuous cement-based composites that simultaneously exhibit enhanced mechanical performance and tunable electrical conductivity. The work integrates principles from materials science, electrochemistry, and civil engineering to develop composites suitable for smart structural applications, including structural health monitoring, self-sensing capabilities, and electromagnetic shielding. The study leverages multiscale characterization techniques to understand the interplay between microstructure, connectivity, and multifunctional properties.
Previous Research
Structural Engineering
Blast Load Effects on Building Structures
Experimental investigation of boiler blast loads on building structures at BUET. Included large- and small-scale specimen testing, dynamic data acquisition, and assessment of structural resilience. Funded by the CASR grant, BUET.
Materials
Fiber-Reinforced & Sustainable Concrete
Extensive experimental study of mechanical behavior of concrete with natural and synthetic fibers — including jute, PVC, banana, and coconut fibers — and supplementary cementitious materials such as fly ash, rice husk ash, sugarcane bagasse ash (SCBA), and wood powder. Published 6+ peer-reviewed journal papers on these topics.
Computational
Numerical Modeling of RC Structures
Developed and validated numerical models of lateral behavior of masonry-infilled RC frames in OpenSees focusing on sliding failure of infill panels, blast-induced column response using Abaqus, and retrofitting of RC columns under lateral loads. Parametric studies informed design recommendations for retrofitting strategies.
Geotechnical
Chemical Stabilization of Expansive Soils
Investigation of the effectiveness of different chemical additives on the stabilization of expansive (swelling) soils, with implications for foundation engineering in regions with problematic soils.
Sustainability
Carbon Emission & Sustainable Construction
Analysis of carbon emissions and sequestration associated with residential building construction, and implementation of BIM for sustainable construction practices in developing countries — with emphasis on economic, environmental, and social aspects.
